AND · 02

By 2070, warming becomes visible.

AND · 03

Emissions change the map.

Under higher emissions, the same future year can show stronger average warming across more of the country.

BUT · 04

But averages hide the days people actually feel.

People do not experience climate as an annual average. They experience it as days when outdoor practice is cancelled, AC runs all night, or outdoor work becomes risky.

BUT · 05

Count extreme hot days instead.

So instead of only asking how much the average temperature changes, we count how many more days cross an extreme heat threshold.

THEREFORE · 06

Extremes reveal a sharper pattern.

Some states gain many more extreme hot days than others, even when average warming looks like a broad national trend.

THEREFORE · 07

Higher emissions sharpen the daily-risk story.

The change is not only a warmer average. It can mean more days when heat crosses a threshold people have to live through.

Takeaway

Average warming describes the climate. Extreme hot days describe the days people live through.

A small average change can still mean many more days above an extreme heat threshold. That is where climate change becomes daily risk.
